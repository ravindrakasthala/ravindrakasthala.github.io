Citrix

Overview

Being online today is taken for granted. Whether you're taking a client call from home, or you're sending out an email on your way to the airport, or you're building an app at your workstation, staying connected is essential. How do you keep apps running across the globe? How do you keep the ball rolling despite outages in some parts of the world? The answer is an Application Delivery Controller(ADC). An ADC is essentially a load balancer that also helps you, monitor, orchestrate, and deploy your apps at ease.

Breif

Obviously, we need to know what we want to achieve with our user experience project. What are our objectives? Why? How we will measure the success of the project? A brief talk about all these things. If we keep our objectives at the most abstract level possible, it gives us the freedom to come up with the best possible solution.
We write all the possible user problems, business problems, and their outcomes in detail. We also talk about stakeholders responsibilities, scope, and timeline of the project.

This is a living document that helps all the stakeholders not to deviate from the goal that we set for the project.

UX Audit

The purpose of the User Experience Audit was to identify and quantify key issues in the Product, in the realm of three categories:
1. Visual Design: color/text hierarchies and layout, alignments and affordances
2. Interaction Design: the path to complete a task
3. Conceptual Design: informational architecture, categories/groupings used

To perform the experience evaluation, we explored the product through the lens of 10 heuristic principles:
1. Visibility of system status
2. Match between system and the real world
3. User control and freedom
4. Consistency and standards
5. Error prevention
6. Recognition rather than recall
7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
10. Help and documentation

UX Ideation

UX Ideation helps us to think about the breadth and depth of the problem. For this project, we conducted a ‘brainstorming sessions’ with all the product managers who involved in this project to understand the problems. Brainstorming aims at the quantity, which later turns into quality. The more ideas a team generates the wider choice it gains. Our session was a goal-oriented and time-bounded. We synthesized the problems and put them in different buckets. We clarified all the questions regarding the project with product managers. As part of the process, we came up with different ways to tackle the problem in an effective way.

Experience Map

It’s important to look at the whole picture before we start building or even designing. A experience map helps us to sketch out the UX and forecast any friction before creating the actual prototype.
A experience map shows the users’ needs, touch points and tasks. It helps us to understand the relation between the different users and their objectives. Having a big picture of our customer’s journey helps us in collaboration between design, tech, and business groups easy and effective. It also helps us to think from our user’s perspective and visualise the friction we have in experience.

Workflows

Workflows talk about the Why. Why is this experience important? Within the agreed scope of work, designers identify key experiences and corresponding user goals.
A workflow also helps us estimate effort and the transition of experience across different parts of the eco-system. For example, a network comprises of SD-WAN, ADC, and Gateway. This helps us plan for the interdependencies better.
Design problems listed against every screen help us keep tabs on important aspects that get lost over the course of a design cycle. They also help us look out for possible design opportunities.
Workflows also help us understand how different users have different layers of interaction with each other. They also facilitate scope discussions with engineers.
